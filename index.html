RNA Polymerase I

rRNA Synthesis · Nucleolus

RNA Polymerase I binds to rDNA in the nucleolus and unwinds the double helix to begin transcription.

It synthesises a long pre-rRNA transcript, which is processed into the rRNAs that form the core of ribosomes.

rDNA — Ribosomal DNA

Ribosomal DNA is present as multiple copies in the nucleolus. RNA Pol I binds to the rDNA promoter, unwinds the double helix, and exposes the template strand for transcription.

›
⚙️

RNA Polymerase I

RNA Polymerase I is found exclusively in the nucleolus. It is responsible for transcribing rDNA into pre-rRNA — the precursor to all ribosomal RNAs. It moves along the template strand and synthesises pre-rRNA continuously.

›
🔗

pre-rRNA Transcript (45S)

The initial transcript is called 45S pre-rRNA. It is a large precursor that is later processed (cleaved) into three functional rRNAs — 28S, 18S, and 5.8S — which form the structural backbone of every ribosome.

›
✨

snoRNPs — Small Nucleolar RNPs

Small nucleolar ribonucleoproteins bind the 45S pre-rRNA after transcription. They guide chemical modifications of specific nucleotides — essential for the pre-rRNA to fold correctly and assemble into functional ribosomes.

›
🔵

18S rRNA

18S rRNA forms the structural core of the small (40S) ribosomal subunit. During translation, it helps position mRNA so that codons can be accurately read by the ribosome.

›
🟠

28S + 5.8S rRNA

28S and 5.8S rRNA are both derived from the 45S pre-rRNA. Together they form the core of the large (60S) ribosomal subunit. The 28S rRNA contains the active site that catalyses peptide bond formation during protein synthesis.

›
🔷

Pre-40S — Small Subunit

The 18S rRNA combines with ribosomal proteins to form the pre-40S particle. It is exported to the cytoplasm, where it completes maturation and joins with the 60S subunit to form a complete 80S ribosome.

›
🔶

Pre-60S — Large Subunit

The 28S, 5.8S, and 5S rRNA assemble with ribosomal proteins to form the pre-60S particle. It is exported from the nucleus and completes its final maturation steps in the cytoplasm.

›
🟢

5S rRNA — RNA Pol III Product

Unlike the other rRNAs, the 5S rRNA is transcribed outside the nucleolus by RNA Polymerase III. It is then imported into the nucleolus and incorporated into the large (60S) ribosomal subunit.

›
🚪

Nuclear Export via NPCs

Pre-ribosomal subunits are too large to diffuse through the nuclear envelope. They are actively transported out through nuclear pore complexes (NPCs) — large protein channels in the nuclear membrane. Final maturation takes place in the cytoplasm.

›
🔵

Nuclear Pore Complex (NPC)

Nuclear pore complexes are large protein channels that span both membranes of the nuclear envelope. They control the selective movement of molecules between the nucleus and cytoplasm. Large pre-ribosomal particles require specific export proteins to pass through.

›
🌐

Cytoplasmic Maturation

Once in the cytoplasm, both subunits complete their final maturation. The small (40S) and large (60S) subunits then join together to form a complete, translation-ready 80S ribosome.

›
🔵

Eukaryotic Cell — Organelles

A eukaryotic cell contains membrane-bound organelles with specialised roles. The rough ER and Golgi process proteins; mitochondria supply energy; the cytoskeleton provides structural support.

Translation — Protein Synthesis

The pre-40S and pre-60S subunits join onto an mRNA to form a complete 80S ribosome. It then reads the mRNA codons to assemble a chain of amino acids — a protein.
